About Me

I am a Computer Engineering student at the University of Massachusetts Amherst with a focus on embedded systems, firmware development, and hardware–software integration. I enjoy building robust end-to-end solutions, from writing low-level drivers to designing custom PCBs and 3D printed enclosures.

Through my personal projects, I have honed my technical expertise and problem-solving abilities. These include developing firmware and designing a custom PCB for a DIY camera using a Teensy 4.1, ArduCam Mega, and an LED display, writing drivers for the BNO055 IMU on a Raspberry Pi Pico, and creating a high-performance terminal text editor in C. I have also built a linear regression model to predict house prices and explored real-time visual processing in C++.

Beyond the classroom, I’ve gained practical experience through student engineering organizations, collaborating with peers to deliver reliable results. Recently, I have been working with my design group on the UMass CubeSat Team to develop a custom battery management system (BMS) and power distribution unit (PDU). This work has included writing a research paper outlining a comprehensive radiation protection plan for the power system in a three year sun synchronous orbit and creating a prototype schematic for the PDU.

I enjoy exploring new advancements in technology, experimenting with innovative ideas, and learning about emerging trends in computer engineering. I’m interested in internship and project opportunities in embedded systems, low-level software, and hardware design where I can continue developing my skills and building reliable, real-world systems.
